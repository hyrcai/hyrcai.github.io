    Overview

    NOVID's technology provides a far-sighted and personalized COVID radar for each user.

    NOVID was founded in March 2020 by world reknowned mathematician Po-Shen Lo, and launched in April 2020 in the App and Play Store. We currently have over a hundred thousand users, as well as various universities and cities using our community tools.

    I joined NOVID's design team in the summer of 2020 as a specialist in motion and visual design. My other two team members are Bennett Huffman, who specializes in UI/UX and web development; and Dean Dijour, who specializes in UI/UX and also leads Android development. I started out by creating onboarding animations, but later branched out to do a little bit of everything, from rehauling the design system to redesigning some in-app flows.

    Design System

    Background

    Before

    Before I joined NOVID, they hadn't yet developed a solid design system, but were in the process of starting one. See some screenshots of NOVID's old app and website below; you can also see the old website here.

    Design System

    Web

    Type

    I helped kick off the new design system by creating a new type system that would work across both platforms and be responsive on web. Using this type system and the pre-existing system for spacing and padding (based on a 4px grid), I redesigned the website, focusing on hierarchy and clarity.

    This redesign was v2 of the website; the current live version is v3, which was designed by Bennett to focus more on storytelling and our new features. You can still see v2 here.

    Design System

    Branding

    Logo & Wordmark

    I refined the logo and wordmark slightly to match the new type system.

    Design System

    App

    Foundation

    Moving on from the website, I started tackling the design system for the app. This included tuning the size of various elements, like buttons; re-illustrating icons to create a consistent icon set; and developing a color system (based on pre-existing brand colors and using the material design system) that meets accessibility standards, ensuring that all type in the app meets at least AA standards for contrast.

    Design System

    App

    Buttons

    Further fleshing out the app design system, I defined the states (neutral, pressed, disabled) for all buttons and inputs for both light and dark mode.

    UI/UX

    App

    Onboarding

    Along with applying new design system styles, I redesigned the onboarding screens. We were experiencing a lot of distrust surrounding our permissions, so I expanded the flow, dedicating one screen to each permission to explain transparently why it was needed (worked a bit on copywriting here as well). I made both full-screen and pop-up iterations; we decided on the popup since it gave users a peek of our interface, and because the module could easily be re-used for other pop-ups in the app once it was developed.

    UI/UX

    App

    Communities

    NOVID was introducing a new feature called communities, which allowed users to create and join virtual communities (such as their university). Community admins can set up daily surveys, send announcements, and view data on how their community is doing through community dashboards online. While my team members focused on the dashboards, I focused on the in-app UI, which was pretty simple since the DS had been established.

    Conclusion

    Takeaways

    NOVID is the most extensive project I've worked on, and I'm so glad that I get to work with such an amazing team on something that has the potential to really help people. I've learned a lot about UI/UX, design systems, working with developers, and accessibility, and really enjoyed working with other designers on a real, live product.

    That being said, there are definitely things I wish I and the team could focuse more on. Since NOVID is a small startup with few people, we move fast and are often stretched thin. This usually means that we have no time for any user testing more in-depth than sending quick polls in Slack. Additionally, although we track some clicks and user activity, we don't have a concrete system of measuring of if our features/designs actually upped user retention or interest. I haven't had much experience with either working with user testing or using statistics as a heuristic for design, but I feel like both are important parts of design in industry; I hope to be able to learn more about both in future positions.
